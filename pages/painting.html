Painting: India's Silent Epics on Canvas

Warli Painting

These paintings, primarily by the Tribal communities of Maharashtra, depict daily life, nature, and tribal rituals using simple geometric shapes in white on a red ochre background.

Madhubani Painting

Originating from the Mithila region of Bihar, these vibrant paintings often feature Hindu deities, nature, and daily life, characterized by intricate line work and bright natural colors.

Mandala Painting

Originating from the Mithila region of Bihar, these vibrant paintings often feature Hindu deities, nature, and daily life, characterized by intricate line work and bright natural colors.

Tanjore Painting

This classical South Indian art form, originating from Thanjavur in Tamil Nadu, is renowned for its rich colors, intricate gesso work creating a 3D effect, and the extensive use of glittering gold foil and embedded precious or semi-precious stones, typically depicting Hindu deities.

Pithora Painting

Created by the Rathwa, Bhil, and Bhilala tribal communities in Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh, and Rajasthan, these ritualistic wall paintings are vibrant depictions of their daily life, mythology, and deities, often featuring horses and created as a votive offering to seek blessings and prosperity.

Kalighat Painting

Kalighat Painting, the distinctive 19th-century art form originated around the Kalighat Kali Temple in Kolkata, West Bengal, characterized by bold outlines, vibrant colors, and simplified figures, and dynamic narrative depicting both Hindu deities and satirical commentary on contemporary social life.
